Timeline of Alfred Hill life

  • He was initially employed as a radio performer and it was in 1950 that he made his debut on the television.
  • On January 15, 1955, he came out with the comedy show titled, 'The Benny Hill Show'.
  • In 1956, he played the role of 'Hugo Dill', a detective who goes on a mission, in the British drama.
  • In 1961, he came out with the indie song titled, ‘Gather in the Mushrooms', which did well on the UK Singles Chart.
  • In 1963, he came out with the short romance song, ‘Harvest Of Love', which he also co-wrote and performed.
  • In 1964, he played the role of ‘Nick Bottom' in the television film that was an adaptation of William Shakespeare's legendary play.
  • In 1964, he began to host his own radio programme titled, 'Benny Hill Time' on the BBC Radio's Light Programme.
  • In 1965, he played the minor role of 'Fire Chief Perkins' in the British triller film.
  • In 1968, he appeared in the British musical film titled, ‘Chitty Chitty Bang Bang', which was based on the Ian Fleming novel.
  • In 1969, he played the role of 'Professor Simon Peach' in the British caper film titled, 'The Italian Job'.
  • In 1971, he came out with the novelty song, 'Ernie (The Fastest Milkman in the West)’, which he wrote and performed.
  • In 1974, he starred in the film, 'The Best of Benny Hill ', which was a spinoff from his television show, 'The Benny Hill Show'.
  • In 1985, a computer game, ‘Benny Hill's Madcap Chase’, which was a ZX Spectrum videogame, featured him.
  • In 1986, he appeared in the video of 'Anything She Does', a song by the British rock band, 'Genesis'.
  • In 1989, his television career came to an end, after the production of 'The Benny Hill Show' was cancelled.
  • In 1991, the last TV work of his career, 'Benny Hill's World Tour: New York!' was released.
